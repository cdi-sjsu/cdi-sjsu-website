San Jose State University

Chip Design Initiative

Open-Source RTL & Silicon Design for Students.

CDI is a student-run SJSU engineering club for learning the full digital design flow: idea, RTL, simulation, synthesis, silicon submission, and board-level bring-up. We meet in campus lab rooms for project nights, design reviews, and beginner-friendly workshops.

Our main focus is TinyTapeout: building real physical ASIC chips with Verilog HDL, open-source EDA tools, and documented design practices. Some projects also include custom PCB layouts when a chip, display, sensor, or demo board needs hardware around it.

What we do

We turn student hardware ideas into working digital designs.

CDI is currently building up to submit student projects to the next TinyTapeout shuttle. The club is focused on learning Verilog, writing readable RTL, simulating designs, reviewing waveforms, and documenting enough that another student can understand and continue the work.

Over the next semester, members can bring small digital logic projects, PSoC demos, FPGA/Vivado experiments, and TinyTapeout-ready ideas into a shared club workflow. A RISC-V CPU is a likely long-term build target, with the summer spent preparing examples, workshops, and public resources so new members can join the work without starting from zero.
